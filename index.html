The Timeline!

sengoku period

Clans

Oda Clan: Led by nobunaga Oda, killed by Mitsuhide Akechi Led a valiant victory at Okehazama. Began to march towards Kyoto. Nagashino was won by the Oda, and the end of the Takeda and the start of the new warfare, muskets. Oda captured Kyoto and Nobunaga Oda began sending forces all over Japan. Nobunaga Oda was betrayed by Mitsuhide Akechi. Oda Clan was split, supported either by Tokugawa and Toyotomi Clan

Tokugawa Clan: Led by Ieyasu Tokugawa, finally unified Japan Freed from Imagawa rule by Oda success in Okehazama. Allied with the Oda assisting in the Oda campaign to kyoto. Was defeated by the Takeda at Mitagawahawa, heavy casualties. After Nobunaga death, conflicts emerge between Hideyoshi and Ieyasu. Became Hideyoshi staunch allies. After Hideyoshi's death, Ieyasu attempt to reunify Japan under his rule Began the Civil War, Eastern and Western Army Eastern Army led by Ieyasu Tokugawa Western Army led by Mitsunari Ishida East Army won and Mitsunari was captured and killed. Ieyasu began to unified Japan, with only the Toyotomi in its way. The siege of Osaka led to the end of Sengoku Jidai.

Takeda Clan: Led by Shingen Takeda, died on the march to Kyoto by sickness A rival between Uesugi Kenshin and Takeda Shingen. Katsuyori took Shingen leadership. Takeda Clan destruction at Nagashino by the Oda and Tokugawa

Uesugi Clan: Led by Kenshin Uesugi Rival against Shingen Takeda Died either by sickness or assassination. Sided with Mitsunari against Ieyasu additional forces towards Sekigahara.

Hojo Clan: Led by Ujiyasu Hojo Final Clan to fall under the Toyotomi control, unifying Japan

Azai Clan: Led by Nagamasa Azai Married to Nobunaga's sister, Oichi Betrayed the Oda at Kanegasaki Forest. Defeated at Anegawa, Azai-Asakura Armies vs Oda-Tokugawa Armies and soon collapse

Hashiba Clan (Toyotomi): Led by Hideyoshi Hashiba Loyal to Nobunaga Oda as a Retainer. Killed Mitsuhide Akechi after Nobunaga death. Unified Japan AFTER Hojo defeat. After Hideyoshi's death, Japan split into a civil war Fought for control by Ieyasu and Mitsunari

Shimazu Clan: Led by Yoshihiro Shimazu Rivals of the Tachibana. Defeated by the Toyotomi Clan. Fought against Ieyasu in Sekigahara. Led a valiant retreat from Sekigahara.

Mori Clan: Led by Motonari Mori Held off against the Oda Army Defeated by the Toyotomi

Date Clan: Led by Masamune Date Sided with Hideyoshi during the siege of Odawara Castle Sided with Ieyasu during the East Vs West.

Imagawa Clan: Led by Yoshimoto Imagawa Formed a three way alliance, Hojo, Takeda, and Imagawa. Died by the Oda ambush at the Battle of Okehazama.

Sanada Clan: Led by Masayuki Sanada, seceded by Nobuyuki Sanada Gain independence after Takeda defeat. Clan was split, Nobuyuki Sanada sided with Ieyasu while Yukimura Sanada sided with Mitsunari. Yukimura died at the Seige of Osaka Castle, ending the reunification of Japan.

Tachibana Clan: Led by Muneshige Tachibana Rivals of the Shimazu. Sided with Mitsunari at Sekigahara.

Chokasaka Clan: Led by Motochika Chokasaka Controlled the island of Chugoku
